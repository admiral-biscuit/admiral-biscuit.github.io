University Education

I studied mathematics at the Humboldt University in Berlin with a focus on differential geometry. In October 2021, I finished my master's degree with a total grade of 1,3.

Master thesis: Dirac Eigenvalues of Homogeneous 3-Spheres

Before that, I completed a bachelor's teaching degree in physics and mathematics (2013 to 2016). I proceeded with the consecutive master's teaching degree as well, but I did not enjoy it at all, so I decided to not finish it and completely drop physics in favour of mathematics. (This turned out to be the correct decision.)

Professional Experience

I am an experienced Kotlin developer.

My main objective is writing highly reliable, maintainable, and testable code. In order to achieve this, I place a strong focus on functional programming methods (e.g. Arrow.kt) combined with a compiler-driven and test-driven developing approach.

I actively engage in my company's competence center reliablity and security by presenting functional programming methods to my colleagues on a regular basis.

Additionally, I sometimes write blog posts about these topics.

Teaching

I enjoy teaching, which is why in addition to my full-time job as a developer, I also work as a maths teacher. (The programming is for the wallet, but the maths is for the heart.)

I have been working as a substitute maths teacher at many different schools in Berlin since I finished school in 2013.

Starting from 2022, I have been leading a yearly working group on algebraic structures for particularly eager 10th-grade students at the Heinrich-Hertz-Gymnasium, which is a special maths school in Berlin. The content of the course is at university level.
